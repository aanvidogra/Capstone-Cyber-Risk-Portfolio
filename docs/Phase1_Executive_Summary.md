# Phase 1 Executive Summary

Phase 1 of the Cyber Risk Portfolio established the foundation for evaluating cybersecurity risk within the fictional organization Northstar Health Payments.

The analysis identified several critical systems that support the company's operations including the billing platform, customer portal, employee endpoints, and payment processing integrations. Because these systems store sensitive billing and financial data, they represent significant targets for cyber threats.

The risk register identified multiple high priority risks including phishing-based account compromise, ransomware attacks, excessive user privileges, and cloud misconfiguration. These risks could lead to operational disruption, financial loss, regulatory consequences, and damage to customer trust.

Mapping the organization's security practices to the NIST Cybersecurity Framework demonstrated how cybersecurity activities align with the Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover functions.

Overall, Phase 1 highlights that cybersecurity risk is closely connected to business performance and operational resilience. Future phases of this project will expand the analysis by developing mitigation strategies, improving governance processes, and strengthening the organization’s security posture.
